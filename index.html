About This Visualization

Overview:

This visualization attempts to explore the question: Do better critical reviews of video games result in increased sales numbers and does a game's age rating play any role in total sales? The slides guide the reader in an attempt to answer this question, along with provide other views of the data so they may reach their own conclusions.

Narrative Visualization Structure:

This visualization uses the hybrid interactive slideshow structure with scenes, annotations, parameters, and triggers on each slide. The javascript framework reveal.js, along with similarly structured charts using d3.js v4 (and no external libraries) are used to provide visual consistency between scenes. You can either use the arrows on the keyboard or click the left/right/down/up arrows on the bottom right of each slide to transition through the trigger points on each scene. The second scene allows the user to click/press the down arrow to drill further down into different groupings of the sales data for further exploration. The arrow buttons in the lower right will blink and move to inform the user that they can be used for navigation.

Scenes:

As mentioned previously, the scenes in this visualization were set up using the reveal.js framework in order to maintain visual consistency and not disorient the reader. The common theme is a few guided annotations for each chart followed by opening up the chart for reader exploration. The scatter plot allows for left/right transitions and the stacked bar charts allow for left/right transitioning to process through annotations in a single chart to the final opening up for exploration. After guiding the reader from the scatter plot to a transformed bar chart representation to more easily see trends (grouped by age rating), the bar chart slide also allows for the reader to drill down (using the up/down arrow or buttons) to view other aggregations of the data - sales by region, console platform, and game genre binned into Metacritic aggregate review bars.

The first scene is a scatterplot showing an overview of the entire dataset that was used for this project - sales data for video games from 1980 to 2016 versus Metacritic aggregate review score, visually grouped by age rating using different colors.

Due to the presence of a few outlier values, a log scale for sales was used to spread out the points for easier viewing.

Manual zooming and filtering is initially disabled while the reader is stepped through some annotations to highlight some trends in the data (using triggers provided by using the left/right arrow keys), but on the final transition for the scatter plot (with an accompanying annotation) the user can zoom (using mouse drags) and filter (clicking on the legend) on their own to explore the data, hovering over the points for detailed tooltip information which provide all of the data provided in this data set.

The second scene is a stacked bar chart collapsing all of the data from the scatter plot of game sales vs. Metacritic score (grouped by age rating) for ease of seeing trends, as there are a very large number of data points in the scatter plot. Filtering is disabled as the reader is guided through a few annotations (triggered and cleared using the left/right arrow keys) until the final transition, where the reader is free to explore the data by hovering over the bars to bring up a tooltip and to click on the legend to filter the data. In this scene, the reader is allowed to drill down (using up/down buttons or keys) to further scenes which closely follow the second scene but group the data by different categories seen in the scatter plot data - sales by region, console platform, and game genre also all binned into Metacritic score ranges as stacked bar charts. The annotations, parameters, and triggers follow from the second scene.

Annotations:

The annotations use combinations of SVG rectangles, circles, ellipses, lines, and arrows drawn using d3 with the similar styling and text size to provide visual consistency in each scene and highlight trends in the data. The annotations in each scene change based on the triggers of transitioning using the arrow keys/clicks, which will clear old annotations and add new ones based on the current view of the data in place (all of the annotations are independent of one another and are controlled by changing opacity on each of the slide transitions). The final annotation of each scene/chart informs the user that they are open to explore the data using the corresponding zooming and filtering options. Hovering over scatter plot points or bars on the bar charts will display tooltips with more data.

Parameters:

The driving parameter in each of the scenes and charts is the groupings of the data in question. In the scatter plot (first scene) and first stacked bar chart (second scene) the parameter is Age Rating using the USâ€™s ESRB rating system (Everyone 6+ years old, Everyone 10+ years old, Teen (13+ years old), Mature (17+ years old), and Adults Only (18+ years old). Using the left/right arrow keys in each chart will auto filter the data and provide annotations to highlight interesting observations before being cleared on the final transition in each chart to allow the reader to manually filter based on the parameter. In the following scenes (reached by pressing/clicking down on the 2nd scene), the parameters are: Sales Region (North America, EU, Japan, and Other), Console Platform (Xbox, Playstation, etc), and Game Genre (Action, Sports, Shooter, etc).

Triggers:

Triggers are implemented in this visualization mainly through the use of the arrow keys/arrow buttons (provided by reveal.js in the lower right of each slide) along with the ability to click on the legends of each chart on the final transition of each scene to filter the data by grouping, which allows for further exploration of the data. The scatter plot triggers filter the sales data by age rating and display annotations on the plot to highlight interesting data. The final transition of the scatterplot in the first scene allows the reader to click and drag to select a window to trigger a zoom in addition to filtering the data through the legend.

The transitions on the bar chart scenes also filter the sales data by the grouping detailed on the chart (switching from a stacked bar chart to a regular bar chart) allowing for observations of trends without obfuscation from the stacking on the chart. The bar chart triggers also present different annotations to highlight observations in the data to the reader. The reveal.js arrows will blink and move to alert the user they are available, and various text annotations in the slide also inform the user how to activate the triggers.

Overview

This scatter plot gives a high level overview, including the entire dataset that was pulled from Kaggle. Due to the wide spread of sales (mostly due to a few outliers), a logarithmic scale is used on the y-axis. Click the right button on the bottom right (or press the right arrow key) to progress, left arrow (or key) to backtrack.

Metacritic scores are retrieved from a website (metacritic.com) that aggregates multiple games journalists' review scores for a game and normalizes them on a scale from 1 to 100. This data is grouped by age rating, which is assigned using the ESRB rating system in use in the USA.

Once the chart indicates you can explore, you will be able to zoom (by clicking and dragging a box with the mouse) and filter the data using the legend. Hover over a data point for more details.
